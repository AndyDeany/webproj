Our Business

Introduction

Holmes Investment Properties was incorporated on 22nd July 2016 in England and Wales as a public limited company with registration number 10292157. Its Registered Office is at 106 Mount Street, London, UK, W1K 2TW.

Principal Activities

Holmes Investment Properties has been established exclusively to fund and build new leisure centres for the operating company known as "David Lloyd’s Adventure Parks" Ltd.

"David Lloyd’s Adventure Parks" Ltd is projected to open twelve venues by 2021 for which HIP will finance the purchase of the land plus develop the infrastructure and shell of the property.

The operating company, "David Lloyd’s Adventure Parks" Ltd will be responsible for the discovery of sites, planning permission, fit out, management and marketing of each centre.

The Market

In 2016, the UK is in the seventh year of economic growth (Bank of England report) and consumers are spending more of their disposable income on leisure-based activities.

This has led to a resurgence from leisure activities as a plethora of diverse new entrants are seeking space in the UK; offering activities such as adventure golf, laser combat and high-wire, zip wires and trampolining.

Specifically, of one of the Adventure Parks leading offerings, trampolining, which was the leading activity at the test concept venue, The International Association of Trampoline Parks (IATP) estimates that by the end of this year there will be more than 550 indoor trampoline parks open worldwide. Compare this to more than 100,000 leisure centres around the globe and the opportunity is huge and will grow fast in the western world over the next 5 years. A tipping point has been reached and the market is ripe for exploitation.

A number of overseas trampolining-led operators have begun to establish themselves although none yet have more than 5 centres. This includes Gravity, Gravity Zone, Gravity Force and Skyzone, who are all on the search for new sites. There are at least 43 trampoline-based clubs, with as many as 19 additional centres planned. The market is small, but growing fast, with the number of venues forecast to grow by 50% in the next year or so, therefore the opportunity is very much of the moment.

Transaction volumes in the core UK leisure markets of leisure parks, restaurants, health & fitness, bingo/bowl and cinemas was £3.29 billion in 2014. Development has been driven by increased consumer spend.

"David Lloyd’s Adventure Parks" proposition

Central to all "David Lloyd’s Adventure Parks" will be a range of adventures and activities beyond the established trampolining concept

"David Lloyd’s Adventure Parks" will include multiple activities that are hard to find in one location but fun for everyone. They will include zip wires, crazy golf, trampolining, dodge ball, soft play and climbing walls to name a few of the proposed activities. The management team believe that "David Lloyd’s Adventure Parks" provide the safest equipment possible and features only the finest designed and manufactured equipment. Activity centres have proved to be a very successful business in the US, which had about forty such attractions in 2011.

The Management, through a study by IATA understands that this number has increased significantly to an estimated three hundred either in operation or under construction.

The management team believe that there is generally an increased emphasis on lifestyle-based activities and the desire to take part in something different, the market for active leisure and lifestyle-oriented entertainment is one of the fastest growing sectors of the leisure industry.
